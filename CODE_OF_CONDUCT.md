# Code of Conduct

This project touches a real, sensitive, and still-developing news story. The bar for participation is straightforward:

- **Discuss the mechanism, not the politics.** Disagreement about the engineering (the field construction, the hashing scheme, the similarity metric) is welcome and expected. Turning issues or PRs into a debate about who's to blame in the underlying controversy is not; take that elsewhere.
- **Be direct, not hostile.** Technical criticism, including "this is wrong and here's why", is welcome and encouraged. Personal attacks, harassment, or discriminatory language are not, and will get a comment removed or a contributor blocked without much back-and-forth.
- **Assume good faith on the first pass.** Most disagreements here are going to be about tradeoffs (constant-time implementations vs. readability, scope of the demo vs. scope of a real system), not bad intent.

Reports of conduct that violates this can be sent to the maintainer via the contact listed on the repository's GitHub profile. There's no formal enforcement ladder here, this is a small demonstration project, but repeated bad-faith behavior will result in being blocked from the repository.
