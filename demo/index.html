Case File No. NEET-UG/2026

The paper was compromised before the first student sat down.

On May 3, 2026, 22.7 lakh students sat the NEET-UG exam. A version of the question paper had already been circulating for close to a month. Three of the four exhibits below are not slides, they are small working programs, real cryptography and real math, running in your browser right now.

Exhibit A Narrative reconstruction

A leak has a shape. This one was insider-shaped.

Every reform on the books assumes theft in transit, someone intercepting a paper on its way to a centre. Investigators believe this one moved differently: written out by someone who already had legitimate access, then scanned and sold as a "guess paper" for weeks before anyone official noticed. This exhibit walks the reported timeline, it is a storytelling aid, not a live system.

Someone with legitimate access to the question bank writes the paper out by hand. No system logs a "leak" yet, because on paper, nothing has left the building.

Exhibit B Real SHA-256, computed live

Diffused, until it isn't.

A Supreme Court bench reviewing the case named the actual mechanism of failure: obligations that aren't fixed on a named person stay, in the Court's own word, "diffused," and diffused accountability isn't accountability at all. Toggle the chain below. In pinned mode, every hash is computed in this page, right now, with the browser's native crypto.subtle.digest, nothing is precomputed.

Six people touch this paper. In this model, none of them are individually traceable.

Edit the Transport Custodian's logged note, then verify. Each signature equals SHA-256(previous hash + role + note + timestamp), so changing one field breaks that hash, and every hash chained after it.

Roles and notes are illustrative placeholders. The hashing itself is genuine SHA-256, not simulated.

Exhibit C Real Shamir's Secret Sharing, 4-of-6

Nothing whole to leak.

If a full paper, or the key that unlocks it, exists in one place, it can be copied by one person, exactly as happened here. Below, a real secret is split across six custodians using Shamir's Secret Sharing over GF(256). Toggle which custodians are cooperating. Closing argument

None of this needs new law. It needs someone to build it.

Custody that can't stay diffused. A secret with nothing whole to steal until enough custodians agree. Detection that doesn't wait for a chemistry teacher to lose sleep over a hunch. Three mechanisms, one supply chain problem.
